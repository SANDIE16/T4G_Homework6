Our Raw Food Products

African Yams

Yam, are native to warmer regions of both hemisphere and several species are cultivated as staple food crops in the tropics. In certain tropical cultures, notably in West Africa and New Guinea, the yam is the primary agricultural commodity and the focal point of elaborate rituals. Yams are consumed as cooked starchy food.

African Plantains

Plantain, major group of banana varieties (genus Musa) that are staple foods in many tropical areas. The edible fruits of plantain bananas has more starch than the common dessert banana and is not eaten raw. Because plantains have the most starch before they ripen,they are usually green,either boiled or fried,in savory dishes. The ripe fruits often sweet and are often cooked with coconut juive or sugar as a flavoring. Plantains may also be dried for later use in cooking or ground for use as a meal,which can be furthur refined to a flour.

African Garden Eggs

African Garden Eggsare a type of eggplant used as a food crop in several countries across Africa. These small,white fruits have a teardrop or roundish shape and are valued for their bitterness. Garden egg is indigenous to sub-Saharan Africa and can be stored for up to three months by allowing it to dry,which is particularly useful in tropical regions where refrigeration may be limited. In Ghana,garden egg is one of the three most consumed vegetable, alongside tomatoes and peppers. It is used in stews and can also be eaten raw. Intwerestenly,the stems and leaves of garden eggs are consumed in some African countries,although not in Ghana. Due to its spongy texture,garden egg is sometimes used as a less expensive meat substitute,as it absorbs ather flavors well.

Okro

Okro AAbelmoschus esculentus, known in some English-speaking countries as lady's fingers, is a flowering plant in the mallow family native to East Africa. In Nigeria, okra soup is a delicacy and is popular amongst Yorubas, Igbos, Efiks, Hausas, and other Nigerian ethnic groups. In Yoruba, it is referred to as obe Ila.
